Part I - February 2019 FordGoBike Trip Data¶

Introduction¶

Ford GoBike is a bike sharing service syatem. The dataset includes information about bike trips covering San Francisco Bay area for the month of February. The bike sharing service is for Subscribers, who pay subscription fee, or Customers, who rent bikes on-the-go. The dataset can be downloaded here

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

The dataset is made of 183,412 bike trips with 16 features namely:

  1. Duration (in seconds)
  2. Start time
  3. End time
  4. Start station id
  5. Start station name
  6. Start station latitude
  7. Start station longitude
  8. End station id
  9. End station name
  10. End station latitude
  11. End station longitude
  12. Bike id
  13. User type (Customer or Subscriber)
  14. Member birth year
  15. Member gender
  16. Bike share for all trip (Yes or No)

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

My interest is to determine when most bikes are rented taking into consideration the age and user type of riders.

Of the features you investigated, were there any unusual distributions? Did you perform any operations on the data to tidy, adjust, or change the form of the data? If so, why did you do this?¶

As regards to distribution of birth year, the maximum year is 2001 (18 years as at 2019). This shows the possibility of an age limit of 18 years for riders. The distribution also showed a minimum year of 1878 (141 years). This entry is an outlier but no operation was performed on it because it was a single entry and would have no effect on the overall analysis. Birth year column was used to craete the additional columns listed below.

- age - was gotten by subtracting birth year from 2019
- age_range - age was grouped as '<20', '21-25', '26-30', '31-35', '36-40', '41-45', '46-50', '51-55', '56-60' and '61+

Date and time was merged in the original dataset and had to be separated to different columns. Additionally, to better understand the when most trips are taken during the week, some columns was created from the start_time column. Their description are:

- start_dates - date trip was commenced
- start_times - time trip commenced
- day_of_week - contains 0-6 which represents Monday-Sunday
- time_of_day - hour trip started using 24-hour time
- time - Night, Morning, Afternoon or Evening

def myCountPlot(df, y, hue, palette=None, order=None, hue_order=None):
    plt.figure(figsize=[14,8])
    sb.countplot(data=df, y=y, hue=hue, edgecolor='black', palette=palette, order=order, hue_order=hue_order)
    y=y.replace('_',' ')
    hue=hue.replace('_',' ')
    plt.title(f'''Distribution of {y} by {hue}'''.title(), fontsize=10, weight='bold')
    plt.ylabel(y.title(), fontsize=10, weight='bold')
    plt.xlabel('Frequency'.title(), fontsize=10, weight='bold')
    sb.despine();

Ford GoBike service is used mostly by Subscribers between 26 and 35 years during the week with peak usage times of 8am and 5pm. With the young age of this group, it shows that the service is used mainly by workers and individuals going to their work or business places in the morning and back home later in the day.

Subscribers make up 89.2% of users in the data while Customers make up 10.8%, indicating that Subscribers use the service more than Customers. Notwithstanding, the average duration of trips embarked on by Customers is twice more than that of Subscribers.
